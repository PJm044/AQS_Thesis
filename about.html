Air Quality Monitoring System

An air quality monitoring system developed for the ULS Caloocan Campus Area, crucial for understanding and addressing local air pollution. This system employs various sensors to measure key pollutants and environmental factors, providing real-time data to assess air quality.

The system helps in:

  • Monitoring current pollution levels.
  • Identifying trends in air quality over time.
  • Raising awareness about local air quality conditions.
  • Providing data for assessing potential health risks.
  • Evaluating the effectiveness of potential mitigation strategies.

Sensor Information

Particulate Matter (PMSA003)

Measures PM1.0, PM2.5 (fine particles), and PM10 (coarse particles), which are major components of haze and significant health concerns.

Carbon Monoxide (MQ7 Module)

Detects Carbon Monoxide (CO), a toxic gas often resulting from incomplete combustion.

Air Quality Level (ZP07-MP503 Module)

Provides a general indication of air quality based on combined sensitivity to various volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and other gases, reported as a pollution level (0-3).
